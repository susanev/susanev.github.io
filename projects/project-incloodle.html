Project Summary

Incloodle is an iPad app that was designed and developed by Kiley Sobel and Kyle Rector at the University of Washington to support children with and without disabilities with inclusion during play. I joined them to help conduct usability studies, evaluate the app, and submit our findings to the 2016 ACM Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI) 2016 conference.

Problem Space

Incloodle is an iPad app designed to allow two children to play inclusively together. Incloodle focuses on social and emotional learning between the children. They together take pictures of themselves, or other objects guided by the app.

Four versions of the app were designed to evaluate the effectiveness of different strategies to encourage play among chidlren with and without disabilities.

Process

We conducted laboratory studies in a large classroom space. We created a central play area, as well as two smaller areas to ask each child questions. More coming soon...

Results

We learned through our labortory study that technology-enforced cooperation was helpful but character-based promoting had little effect.
