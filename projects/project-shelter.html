Project Summary

Over a 36-hour period we created an interactive installation to evoke emotion and motivate community action to address police violence in America.

On November 22, 2014, Tamir Rice a 12-year-old child was shot by a police officer while playing in a gazebo at a park in Cleveland, Ohio. In September of 2016, the gazebo was taken down and sent to Chicagoâ€™s Stony Island Arts Bank.

We wanted to create an interactive exhibit that evoked strong emotions and challenged people to think deeply about police killings. We created our own gazebo using wood, fabric, and rope. We then projected down a series of police shooting descriptions taken from Mapping Police Violence. When people approached or walked inside the gazebo the text descriptions blacked out, obstructing the viewers ability to read the description.

Problem Space

How might we design an interactive installation that evokes feelings and motivates community action to address police violence in America?

Process

As a team we first talked about the experience we wanted to create and brainstormed ways in which to construct the gazebo. Helen then led the material gathering and construction of the gazebo while I worked on the codebase. Using git along the way to track my progress, I started by cleaning the data and writing JavaScript code to display the names and descriptions from the data.

Next, I added code to black out random words in the description. I did this strategically, saving specific words until the end to grab the attention of our viewers. Once the JavaScript code was working. I setup an Arduino and 6 InfraRed Motion Sensors, followed by creating a node.js server to send the data from the sensors to my JavaScript.

Hours before our opening I wired all the sensors up and connected the projector. We ran out of time for any last-minute adjustments, but I plan to continue to work on this project.

Results

We found the project to be a success at raising awareness and evoking emotions. Many people mentioned how "frustrated" they were with not being able to read the descriptions, exactly the kind of experience we were trying to create.

Though the gazebo we built was taken down after the night of our show, I have plans to continue the code base and create a web-experience that evokes similar emotions.
